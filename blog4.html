Yonehara-shi, Shiga Prefecture

Stepping into Yonehara-shi in Shiga Prefecture felt like stepping back in time. The renovated traditional Japanese house exuded an air of nostalgia and warmth. As I walked through the doors, memories of my grandmother's house flooded my mind, and I couldn't help but feel a sense of comfort and familiarity.

During my stay, I immersed myself in the tranquility of the countryside. The days were filled with exploring the nearby Kadairaji Castle and its enchanting gardens. I marveled at the preserved goodness of the old private houses, appreciating the intricate details and craftsmanship that went into their construction.

To indulge in the local culture, I visited the Ibukiyama Cultural Museum, where I learned about the rich history and traditions of the region. The nearby Ibuki Yakusa-no-Yu hot springs provided a blissful retreat, soothing my tired muscles and rejuvenating my spirit.

As I ventured further afield, I discovered the beauty of the Sekigahara Ancient Battlefield Memorial Hall—a place where history came alive. The relay point for historical tours, Yonehara-shi served as the perfect base for exploring the Sekigahara-Nagahama-Fukui region, with its captivating landscapes and tales of the past.

Back at Yonehara-shi, the evenings were spent in quiet contemplation. I sat by the window, watching as the changing seasons painted the countryside in hues of gold and crimson. It was a reminder of the cyclical nature of life and the beauty that can be found in every moment.

Leaving Yonehara-shi was like saying goodbye to a cherished part of my heritage. The memories of my time spent there will forever hold a special place in my heart—a testament to the power of reconnecting with our roots and finding solace in the simplicity of life.
